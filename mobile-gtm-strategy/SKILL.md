# Mobile GTM Strategy

> From app launch to scaled growth: ICP definition, positioning, ASO,
> user acquisition, and a sustainable growth framework.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

No marketing decision is made before defining who you are trying to win.

### Demographic Framework

An ICP for a mobile application is defined across the following dimensions. The age range and device preference of the target audience establish the tone and technical complexity of the product. The usage context describes the environment in which the app is opened — during a commute, in a workplace without a desktop computer, or during leisure time. Pain point urgency quantifies how much time or money the user currently spends on the problem. Where this figure is low, willingness to pay will be low as well.

### ICP Validation Criteria

An ICP definition passes validation when at least twelve of the first twenty paying users match the profile, when users in this segment are already using competing applications, and when keywords targeting this audience show meaningful search volume in the App Store or Google Play.

## 2. App Positioning

### April Dunford Method (Adapted for Mobile)

**Step 1 — List real alternatives.** What would users do if this app did not exist? Possibilities include another app, a website, a manual workaround, or doing nothing at all.

**Step 2 — Identify differentiating features.** Which capabilities are genuinely different from those alternatives — difficult or impossible to copy in the short term?

**Step 3 — Translate to value.** What does each differentiator concretely deliver for the user? Avoid internal language such as "40% faster synchronisation"; instead, say "Have your files ready before the meeting in three taps."

**Step 4 — Identify who cares most.** Who values this most, and are they the person making the purchase decision?

### Positioning Statement Template

```
[App name] is a [category] application that helps [target user]
accomplish [primary job] more effectively.
Unlike [alternative / competitor], [distinguishing difference]
delivers [measurable outcome].
```

## 3. App Store Optimisation (ASO)

ASO comes before paid user acquisition — it is a free, sustainable channel.

**Title and subtitle.** The iOS App Store title is limited to 30 characters. The highest-volume search keyword belongs here. The subtitle carries the second-priority keyword and a value proposition. Google Play allows 30 characters for the title and 80 for the short description.

**Description structure.** The first three lines appear before the "Read more" button. Roughly 80% of users never scroll past this point. A clear value proposition and a call to action must appear in this section.

**Screenshots.** Screenshots have the greatest influence on conversion. The recommended sequence is: the first image presents the primary benefit, the second shows how the app works, and the third provides social proof. Text overlays should be short and in a large font size.

**Keyword research.** Tools such as AppFollow, Sensor Tower, data.ai, and AppFigures support keyword discovery. The strategic target is the intersection of high search volume and low competition. Category-level, non-branded keywords are prioritised at the outset.

**Review and response strategy.** Every negative review should receive a response within 24 to 48 hours confirming that the issue has been addressed. The best moment to request a review from a satisfied user is immediately after a successful action — a completed purchase, a passed level, or an achieved goal.

## 4. 90-Day Launch Plan

### Pre-Launch (Days −30 to 0)

The objective of this phase is to warm the audience and build an initial user base before public release. A waitlist is established with a referral system offering early access as the incentive. Content production begins, addressing the problem space through blog posts and short-form video. Relationships with relevant influencers and content creators are initiated early. Beta access is opened to 50–200 users via TestFlight or the Google Play Internal Track.

### Launch Week (Days 1–7)

A Product Hunt launch requires a network capable of generating at least 50 upvotes; this preparation should be complete before launch day. An announcement email is sent to the full waitlist. A consistent, high-frequency social media publishing schedule is executed. Influencer content is timed to coincide with this window.

### Growth Phase (Days 8–90)

The first seven days of data are analysed to identify which acquisition channel delivered the lowest CPI and the highest retention. Budget is shifted towards that channel. A/B testing begins on App Store screenshots, the onboarding flow, and notification copy. Early reviews carry significant weight for App Store visibility and should be actively collected during this period.

## 5. User Acquisition Channels

### Organic Channels (Start Here)

**Content marketing.** SEO-aligned content is produced around topics that the target audience actively searches for. The application is not promoted directly; the problem is solved, and the app is presented as part of that solution.

**Community.** Reddit, Facebook Groups, Discord servers, and forum platforms are engaged with genuine value first. The application is introduced organically once credibility is established.

**Referral programme.** Acquiring users from existing users is the lowest-cost acquisition path. Incentives must be bilateral — both the referrer and the referred user should receive something of value.

### Paid Channels (After the Organic Base Is Established)

Apple Search Ads reach users with high purchase intent and close proximity to the target action, but they are expensive and require ongoing keyword monitoring. Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads offer broad audiences, strong retargeting, and lookalike audience capabilities, but creative fatigue sets in quickly. TikTok ads offer a high viral potential for audiences aged 18–34 and relatively low CPMs, though the content production burden is significant. Google UAC provides cross-platform reach with automated optimisation, at the cost of reduced transparency and control.

### Target Benchmarks

Industry reference values for non-gaming utility applications place CPI (cost per install) in the range of $1–$5. Day-1 retention of 25–35%, Day-7 retention of 10–15%, and Day-30 retention of 5–10% are standard benchmarks. An LTV:CAC ratio of at least 3:1 is considered healthy.

## 6. Pricing Strategy

**Freemium.** Core features are free; premium features are unlocked via subscription or a one-time purchase. This is the dominant model across most categories. Typical conversion rates fall between 2% and 5%.

**Subscription.** Monthly or annual payment. Offering an annual plan increases LTV and is typically presented at a 30–50% discount to the monthly equivalent. Apple and Google take a 30% commission in year one, reducing to 15% from year two onwards.

**One-time purchase.** Still viable for simple utility apps, but limits the development sustainability of ongoing updates.

**Consumable in-app purchase.** Used in games and creative applications via token or credit systems.

For price discovery, A/B testing is the most reliable approach. App Store Connect permits different price points to be offered in different regions, and the perceived value of an annual plan should be presented as 20–30% below the monthly rate multiplied by twelve.

## 8. Competitor Analysis Framework

For each competitor, four areas are examined. The App Store positioning review covers the title, subtitle, and first three screenshots to determine the primary message. The review pattern analysis identifies the most frequently mentioned positives and negatives — these often reveal product gaps. The keyword overlap analysis identifies terms the competitor already ranks for and where they are weak. The update frequency review examines the last six months of release notes to understand what the competitor is prioritising.
